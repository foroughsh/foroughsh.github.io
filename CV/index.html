I was particularly interested in bridging theory and practice, addressing real-world constraints such as scalability, reliability, and performance. Through close collaboration with academic and industrial partners, I contributed to research aimed at building autonomous and self-managing systems.

  • performance prediction and analysis of cloud and distributed systems
  • machine learning for autonomous and self-managing systems
  • modeling and monitoring key performance indicators (KPIs) and SLAs
  • applied research with real-world deployment considerations

During my time at Informatics Service Corporation, I worked as a researcher and developer in the R&D group, contributing to large-scale, mission-critical systems in the banking and networking domains. My work ranged from developing and maintaining Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) and digital signing systems for the Central Bank of Iran to designing embedded routing solutions, performing data analysis to improve banking services, and building expert systems for network troubleshooting. This role gave me strong hands-on experience in system-level engineering, security, and applied problem-solving in real production environments.

  • backend and systems development using C/C++, Java, Python, and Linux-based environments
  • design and operation of secure networked systems, including PKI, digital signing, and IP-based protocols
  • data analysis and scripting for system monitoring and service improvement
  • hands-on experience with network engineering, troubleshooting, and embedded systems
